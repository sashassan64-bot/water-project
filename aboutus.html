Our Story

H2O Aid was estabished in the covid seasons in , gayaza in early 2019, when a group of engineers, teachers, and mothers came together after a devastating drought claimed dozens of lives—including children who drank from contaminated ponds.

Our founder, Sas Hassan, a civil engineer who grew up carrying jerrycans 10 kilometers to school, made a promise: “No child in Somalia should die because they drank dirty water.”

With just $3,200 raised from family and friends, we drilled our first well in MASAKA village. On the day the water flowed—clear, cool, and safe—300 villagers wept. That moment became our mission.

Today, H2O Aid operates in 6 regions: JINJA, SOROTI, NEBI, KOBOKO, MASAKA, and KYOTERA. We are 100% Somali-staffed, community-driven, and laser-focused on sustainable water solutions.

Success Stories

Amina’s Village – Garowe, Somalia

Before H2O Aid, Amina walked 8 km daily with her 5-year-old brother on her back to fetch water from a drying riverbed. The water was brown and made her family sick. In 2024, we drilled a 120-meter borehole with a solar pump. Now, 1,200 people have water within 200 meters of their homes. Amina is back in school—and she wants to be an engineer.

St. Mary’s School – Jowhar

This school had no toilets. Girls stayed home during menstruation. We built gender-separated latrines, handwashing stations, and a 10,000-liter rainwater tank. Enrollment increased by 40%, and the school now teaches hygiene to neighboring villages.

The Farmers of Balcad

Drought destroyed crops. We installed drip irrigation from a nearby well. Farmers now grow tomatoes, onions, and lemons year-round. One cooperative earned $12,000 last season—enough to send 15 kids to high school.
