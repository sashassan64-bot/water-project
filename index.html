Why Water Matters

Health

Contaminated water causes 80% of all diseases in developing regions. Diarrhea alone kills 1,400 children under 5 every day. A single clean water point can reduce child mortality by up to 65%.

Education

Girls often miss school to fetch water—walking 6 kilometers daily. With a well nearby, attendance rises by 25%, and girls are more likely to complete secondary education.

Economy

Families spend 4–6 hours a day collecting water. Access to clean water frees time for farming, small businesses, and income generation—lifting households out of poverty.

Environment

Our wells reduce pressure on rivers and wetlands. Rainwater systems prevent soil erosion. And every latrine we build stops human waste from polluting local ecosystems.
